Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers about borrowing, lending, groups, and more

How do I borrow on M‑Pesewa?

Register as a borrower, join a group, select an emergency loan category, add it to your cart. Lenders in your group will see your request and may offer to lend. You cannot choose a lender—lenders choose you based on your rating and trustworthiness.

What loan categories are available?

There are 16 emergency categories: Transport (Fare), Data/Airtime, Cooking Gas, Food, Urgent Repairs (Credo), Water Bill, Fuel, Vehicle Repair, Medicine, Electricity Tokens, School Fees, TV Subscription, Daily Sales Advance, Working Capital Advance, Wifi, and Advance Loan.

What are the loan terms?

Maximum 7 days repayment, 10% interest per week. Daily partial repayments allowed. After 7 days, a 5% daily penalty applies on the outstanding balance. Default occurs after 2 months of non‑payment.

Can I borrow from multiple groups?

Yes, you can belong to up to 4 groups (with good rating) and borrow one loan per group at a time, in different categories.

Do borrowers pay subscription fees?

No. Borrowers pay no subscription fees (except in higher tiers where a small borrower fee may apply for access to larger loans).

How do I become a lender?

Register as a lender, choose a subscription tier (Basic, Premium, Super, or Lender of Lenders), pay the subscription fee via M‑Pesa Till, then join a group. Once your subscription is active, you can view borrower loan requests in your group and select whom to lend to.

What are the subscription tiers?

Basic (≤ 1,500/week), Premium (≤ 5,000/week), Super (≤ 20,000/week), Lender of Lenders (≤ 50,000/week). Each has monthly, bi‑annual, and annual payment options. Subscriptions expire on the 28th of each month.

How do I get repaid?

Repayments happen off‑platform (M‑Pesa, bank, cash). Borrowers send you money directly. You then manually update the ledger in your dashboard to reflect the repayment.

Can I lend across groups or countries?

No. Lending is strictly within your group and country. No cross‑group or cross‑country lending is allowed.

What if a borrower defaults?

After 2 months of non‑payment, the borrower is automatically blacklisted. You can report the default, but only the Platform Admin can remove the blacklist after full repayment.

What is a group?

A group is a trusted circle of people (family, church, profession, community) within one country. Groups have 5‑1000 members, one Admin/Founder, and are invitation‑only.

How do I join a group?

You must be invited by the group Admin or referred by at least two existing members (guarantors). You cannot join a group without an invitation or referral.

Can I create a group?

Yes, any registered user can create a group. You become the Admin/Founder and must invite the first 5 members to activate the group.

How many groups can I join?

Borrowers can join up to 4 groups (with good rating). Lenders can join unlimited groups but can only lend within each group separately.

Are groups country‑locked?

Yes. A group exists only in one country and cannot include members from other countries.

What is a ledger?

A ledger is an active loan record. When you lend to a borrower, a ledger is automatically created under your lender profile. It tracks borrower details, amount, dates, interest, penalties, and status.

Who updates the ledger?

Lenders update ledgers manually when repayments are received. Platform Admin can override entries if needed.

How does the rating system work?

Lenders rate borrowers (1‑5 stars) after loan repayment. The average rating is visible on the borrower’s profile and affects their ability to join more groups and get selected for loans.

What is the blacklist?

Borrowers who default for 2+ months are blacklisted. The blacklist badge appears on their profile across all groups, blocking new loans and group joins. Only Platform Admin can remove it after full repayment.

Which countries are supported?

Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, Somalia, South Sudan, Ethiopia, DRC, Nigeria, South Africa, Ghana.

Does M‑Pesewa handle money?

No. All loans are disbursed and repaid off‑platform (M‑Pesa, bank, cash). M‑Pesewa only manages trust, groups, ledgers, reputation, and subscriptions.

How does the platform make money?

Only from lender subscriptions. Borrowers pay no fees (except in higher tiers where a small borrower fee may apply).

Is there a mobile app?

This is a Progressive Web App (PWA). You can install it on your phone’s home screen for an app‑like experience. It works offline and sends push notifications.

How do I contact support?

Use the contact form on the Contact page. Each country also has specific contact details in its country page footer.
